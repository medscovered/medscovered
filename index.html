What are PBMs, and why do they matter?

Pharmacy Benefit Managers, or PBMs, are companies that sit between your insurance plan and your pharmacy. On paper, they negotiate drug prices on behalf of insurers. In practice, the way they operate often results in higher costs for patients and less transparency about where the money actually goes.

The three largest PBMs, Express Scripts, CVS Caremark, and OptumRx, together manage pharmacy benefits for most Americans. Because they control so much of the market, they have enormous influence over which drugs get covered, at what price, and at which pharmacies.

How PBMs affect your prescription costs

When a drug manufacturer pays a PBM a rebate to get their drug on a preferred formulary tier, the PBM doesn't always pass those savings to patients. Instead, patients may pay high copays for drugs that the insurer is actually getting a large rebate on. The net result: you pay more than necessary at the pharmacy counter, and it's very difficult to know why.

PBMs also use practices like spread pricing, charging insurers more for a drug than they pay the pharmacy, and keeping the difference. This is a significant source of PBM revenue that patients and employers rarely see.

The Federal Trade Commission released a report in 2024 finding that the largest PBMs were inflating drug costs and steering patients toward their own affiliated pharmacies, even when cheaper options were available.

What's being done about it

PBM reform has rare bipartisan support in Congress. Both Republican and Democratic legislators have introduced bills requiring greater transparency in rebate reporting, limiting spread pricing in Medicaid, and preventing PBMs from favoring their own affiliated pharmacies. Several states have passed their own PBM transparency laws. The process is slow, but momentum is real.

What you can do right now

Use Cost Plus Drugs

Mark Cuban's pharmacy bypasses PBMs entirely. Transparent pricing, no middleman. Often dramatically cheaper than going through insurance.

Check GoodRx first

Sometimes the cash price with a GoodRx coupon is lower than your insurance copay. Always compare before paying.

Ask for generics

Generic drugs have the same active ingredients as brand names. They bypass many of the formulary games PBMs play with brand manufacturers.

Apply for PAPs

Patient Assistance Programs from manufacturers provide free or reduced-cost medications directly, completely outside the PBM system.

Use this database

MedsCovered exists specifically to help you find every option that routes around the PBM model. Search any medication to get started.

Talk to your doctor

Ask your physician to prescribe the most affordable equivalent. Therapeutic substitution to a cheaper drug in the same class is often clinically appropriate.
